Retatrutide FDA Approval Timeline: What to Expect and When

April 13, 2026  ·  5 min read

Only TRIUMPH-4 has reported Phase 3 results to date. Seven additional Phase 3 trials in the TRIUMPH program are expected to complete in 2026, after which Eli Lilly is expected to submit a New Drug Application (NDA) to the FDA — likely late 2026 or early 2027. Here's the regulatory roadmap and what each milestone means for patients.

The FDA Drug Approval Process, Briefly

For a drug to receive FDA approval, the manufacturer must complete preclinical testing, three phases of clinical trials, and then submit an NDA containing all accumulated data. The FDA reviews the NDA and makes an approval decision. For a drug like retatrutide — treating a serious condition with significant unmet need — the FDA may grant Priority Review, which shortens the standard 12-month review window to 6 months.

Phase 3 TRIUMPH Trials

TRIUMPH-4 has reported results (28.7% mean weight loss at 68 weeks). Seven additional Phase 3 trials in the TRIUMPH program are ongoing, with readouts expected throughout 2026.

NDA Submission

Seven additional TRIUMPH Phase 3 trials are expected to report results in 2026. Once those readouts are complete, Eli Lilly is expected to compile and submit the New Drug Application to the FDA.

Pending
FDA Review Period

The FDA reviews all clinical, manufacturing, and safety data. Standard review is 10–12 months; Priority Review is approximately 6 months. The FDA may request additional data (Complete Response Letter) which can extend the timeline.

Pending
Advisory Committee Meeting (if convened)

The FDA may convene an advisory committee of outside experts to review the application and provide a non-binding recommendation. Not required for all drugs.

Pending
FDA Decision (PDUFA Date)

The FDA issues an approval, a Complete Response Letter requesting additional data, or a rejection. Based on submission timing and standard review, the expected PDUFA date is 2026–2027.

Post-Approval
Commercial Launch & Prescriber Access

Following approval, Eli Lilly would begin commercial manufacturing and distribution. Access through prescribers and specialty pharmacies typically begins within weeks to months of approval.

What Could Delay Approval?

Several factors can extend the typical timeline:

  • Complete Response Letter (CRL): If the FDA requests additional data or manufacturing information, Lilly must respond before a final decision is made.
  • Manufacturing inspection issues: The FDA inspects manufacturing facilities as part of the NDA review. Any findings can delay approval.
  • Labeling negotiations: The FDA and Lilly negotiate the exact language of the prescribing information, including indications, contraindications, and warnings.
  • Risk Evaluation and Mitigation Strategy (REMS): If the FDA requires a REMS program (a safety program restricting how the drug is prescribed or dispensed), additional setup time is required before launch.

The obesity drug class has historically received relatively smooth FDA review. Tirzepatide (Mounjaro) was approved in May 2022 for diabetes and in November 2023 for obesity. Semaglutide (Ozempic/Wegovy) has a similar track record. This regulatory precedent is favorable for retatrutide's path.

What Approval Means for Patients

FDA approval would allow Eli Lilly to market and sell retatrutide in the United States. Physicians could then prescribe it for approved indications. Insurance coverage — a major factor in access for GLP-1 drugs generally — would be determined separately by each payer. Commercial and Medicare/Medicaid coverage decisions often lag behind approval by months to years, which is why waitlists like glp3md focus on physician-supervised access rather than insurance-dependent pathways.
